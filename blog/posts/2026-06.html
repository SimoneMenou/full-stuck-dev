10/2 things I hate about LinkedIn

Title nods to the movie. The math nods to my patience: I aimed for ten, kept the five that actually bothered me most.

  1. Posts crammed with emojis. 🚀 ✨ 🎯. Bullet formatting, a strangely templated tone. Half my feed is ChatGPT in a trench coat. Including, when I was new and excited, mine.
  2. The blurred stalkers. Without Premium, the people who viewed your profile show up as faceless, blurred-out silhouettes you can tell someone's watching, just not who. A little creepy.
  3. The cringeworthy poetic posts. "AI is like rain: both fall from the sky, both can flood your code." Forced metaphors dressed as weird haiku, bordering on hallucinated AI. Storytelling is fine. But management poetry? A bit too much.
  4. The "Engineer" suffix mania. Happiness Engineer. Customer Success Engineer. Growth Engineer. Vibe Engineer. Cool, but what is this engineer mania?
  5. The work anniversary posts. "Celebrating 3 years at [Company]! What a journey 🎉". Facebook birthday energy, work edition. LinkedIn even drafts the post for you.

My first post since I got hired? Full of 🚀 ✨ (point #1, guilty as charged). Half of 10 points, because writing ten started to feel a lot like the kind of content I criticize here. Also because I have other things to do, and... anyway. One last confession: as a developer and die-hard meme lover, LinkedIn memes?
