10/2 things I hate about LinkedIn

Title nods to the movie. The math nods to my patience: I aimed for ten, kept the five that actually bothered me most.

  1. Posts crammed with emojis. 🚀 ✨ 🎯. Bullet formatting, a strangely templated tone. Half my feed is ChatGPT in a trench coat. Including, when I was new and excited, mine.
  2. The bot-vs-human DMs. Half the recruitment messages are templates with your first name swapped in. You never quite know who, or what, you're replying to.
  3. The cringeworthy poetic posts. "AI is like rain: both fall from the sky, both can flood your code." Forced metaphors dressed as weird haiku, bordering on hallucinated AI. Storytelling is fine. But management poetry? A bit too much.
  4. The "Engineer" suffix mania. Happiness Engineer. Customer Success Engineer. Growth Engineer. Vibe Engineer. Cool, but what is this engineer mania? Nobody's a janitor anymore, just a Floor Care Engineer.
  5. The work anniversary posts. "Celebrating 3 years at [Company]! What a journey 🎉". Facebook birthday energy, work edition. LinkedIn even drafts the post for you. A strange ritual of public loyalty.

My first post since I got hired? Full of 🚀 ✨ (point #1, guilty as charged). Half of 10 points, because writing ten started to feel a lot like the kind of content I criticize here. Also because I have other things to do, and... anyway. One last confession: as a developer and die-hard meme lover, LinkedIn memes? Honestly the best for me, hahaha.
