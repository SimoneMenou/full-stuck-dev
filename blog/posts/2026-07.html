10/2 things I love about LinkedIn

Honest counterpart to June. Same platform, other side of the coin. Same math: aimed for ten, kept the five that actually matter.

  1. The honest market signal. Like the job requirements or not, they tell you what companies actually want right now. Free market research.
  2. Connections that actually pay off. People you worked with three years ago see you exist again. They recommend you, intro you, sometimes hire you. The mutual aid runs quietly under the noise.
  3. Access to people you'd never reach otherwise. A clear DM to a senior dev, an engineering manager, a founder. It works. Sometimes more accessible than real life: no awkward party, no business card stuck in a pocket.
  4. Free learning, two ways. LinkedIn Learning is genuinely good. And once you curate your feed (mute the noise, follow the right handful of people), the timeline becomes a tech-thread firehose. Two ways to upskill without paying.
  5. Company research before applying. Headcount, recent hires, who left, who runs what team. All public. You walk into interviews knowing the room.

None of this cancels June. The platform is exhausting and useful at the same time. Both are true. That's also the trap.
