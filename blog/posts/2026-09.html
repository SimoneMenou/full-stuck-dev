10/2 things I love about becoming a dev

Honest counterpart to August. Same career-changer perspective, this time the good parts. Five reasons I'd still do it again.

  1. Mixing art into the code. The design sensibility, the visual instincts, the way you compose layouts in your head, they don't disappear, they translate. Code is a creative medium, and every previous form you worked in adds vocabulary.
  2. Expressing things you couldn't say before. Suddenly you can make the idea exist. A small site, a tool, a script that automates the thing you were doing by hand. The gap between "I have an idea" and "here it is" shrinks dramatically.
  3. The VIP pass behind the glamorous concepts. You stop seeing tech as a glossy black box. You see the wires. You understand why the app froze, why the page is slow, why the form submitted to the wrong endpoint. The trick stops fooling you. You become the one who knows how the trick works.
  4. Practice as brain training. Solving problems daily is the kind of cognitive workout you didn't know you needed. Patterns stick. You start seeing structure everywhere, in code and outside of it. The brain muscle is real.
  5. Your past life counts more than you thought. Production, events, management, design, whatever shaped you before, it becomes an unfair advantage. You understand stakeholders. You explain things in language people actually use. You ship under pressure because you've shipped under worse. The career change isn't a reset. It's compound interest on everything you already knew.

I switched careers thinking I'd start from zero. I didn't. Everything I learned before came with me, just renamed. The art is still art. The expression is still expression. The brain is still mine. The only thing that changed is what I can build with it.
